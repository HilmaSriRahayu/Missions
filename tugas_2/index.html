Designing for Augmented Reality

The definition of augment is to "make (something) greater by adding to it." Augmented Reality (AR) is technology that sets out to do just that by adding a layer of data over the physical world. AR can be experienced in several ways, ranging from smartphones to wearable headsets like the HoloLens.

Design System Fundamentals

Design systems create a bridge between designers and developers. They describe the rules, constraints, and principles of your company's design language. If you've ever visited New York City, there's a good chance you've benefited from the subway design system to navigate around—officially named the New York Transit Authority graphic standards manual, designed by the infamous Massimo Vignelli.

What if you knew exactly what product to make for your users? Design sprints are the fastest way to learn what your users want without having to actually build and launch. The goal of a design sprint is to answer a focused business question by building a prototype and testing with users.
